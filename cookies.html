Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how Ronai Labs may use cookies and similar technologies on the CompanionAI website and, where relevant, within related online services.

This template is not legal advice. Review it for your actual implementation and legal requirements before publishing.

1. Overview

Cookies are small text files stored on a device when a website is visited. Similar technologies may include local storage, software development kit identifiers, pixels, tags, and analytics technologies that help us understand performance, remember preferences, and improve the service.

2. What cookies or similar technologies may be used

  • Authentication technologies: to sign users in and keep sessions secure.
  • Preference technologies: to remember language, consent choices, or display settings.
  • Analytics technologies: to understand visits, feature usage, traffic sources, and technical performance.
  • Security technologies: to detect suspicious activity, enforce protections, and prevent abuse.
  • Functionality technologies: to support core website behavior and improve usability.

3. Why we use these technologies

  • to operate and secure the website;
  • to remember preferences and settings;
  • to understand how visitors use the site;
  • to troubleshoot problems and improve performance;
  • to measure the effectiveness of pages or support flows where permitted.

4. Categories of cookies

Strictly necessary

These are required for core website functions such as page delivery, basic security, and session management. The website may not function correctly without them.

Functional or preference cookies

These help remember settings such as language, consent choices, or user interface preferences.

Analytics cookies

These help us understand how the website is used, such as which pages are visited and how visitors interact with content. Analytics may be provided by us or trusted third-party providers.

Authentication and account-related technologies

If the website later includes account access or support portal features, authentication technologies may be used to maintain secure sessions and protect accounts.

5. Third-party technologies

Some technologies may be set or managed by third-party service providers that support hosting, analytics, security, customer support, or related functions. Their handling of data may also be subject to their own privacy terms and notices.

6. User control options

Users may be able to control cookies and similar technologies in several ways, depending on local law and how the site is configured:

  • through browser settings that block or delete cookies;
  • through any cookie banner or preference center we may provide;
  • through device-level privacy or tracking controls where supported.

Blocking some technologies may affect the website’s functionality, security, or performance.

7. Updates to this Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected by updating the “Last updated” date above and, where appropriate, by providing additional notice.
